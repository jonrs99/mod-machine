01The studio

p. 04About · Process · People

One workbench. Six consoles.
No shortcuts.

Mod Machine is a one-person workshop dedicated to keeping Nintendo's portable line alive. The catalog covers every handheld from the 1989 DMG-01 through the 2005 Game Boy Micro — and only those. Doing one thing well, slowly.

Every device that comes through the shop is opened, photographed, cleaned, tested, and documented. If the original screws stripped before they got here, you'll get a note in the return packet.

Mods are layered, not stacked. An IPS retrofit gets paired with a properly-rated regulator and a clean ground. A USB-C port is fused before it leaves. A bivert chip is socketed so the next owner can pull it without a heat gun. Decisions are made with the next ten years in mind, not the next ten minutes.

Commissions take 7 to 18 days depending on parts. Repairs are quoted before any work begins.
